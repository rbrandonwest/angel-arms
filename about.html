About us

A ministry of presence, dignity, and steady forward motion.

Faith Forward Ministries is a faith-based human services facility serving Westmoreland County, Indiana County, and the surrounding areas. Angel Arms is our extension into the most vulnerable moments of new life. Together, we meet our neighbors where they are.

Our story

Building hope, one goal at a time — since 2013.

Faith Forward Ministries was founded with a single conviction: that no person should have to face crisis alone. From counseling rooms in Latrobe to family resource hubs in Blairsville, our staff and volunteers have spent more than a decade walking with neighbors through addiction, unemployment, incarceration, food insecurity, and the heartbreak of family separation.

Out of that work — and the stories we've witnessed — Angel Arms was born. As more infants entered our community already carrying the weight of their parents' substance exposure, we knew we had to extend care to the very beginning of life. Today, Angel Arms provides a short-term safe atmosphere for infants and children, while continuing to support birth parents and caregivers in their own recovery journeys.

We are guided by Christian compassion, sustained by donations, and committed to the truth that everyone — no matter the circumstance — is worthy of dignity and a brighter path forward.

Mission statements

Two ministries, one calling.

Faith Forward Ministries

"Faith Forward Ministries exists to serve children and families in crisis through compassionate counseling, casework, outreach, and special interest groups. We are dedicated to meeting basic needs with food assistance, emergency supports, and community programs, in partnership with local organizations."

Our ministry is sustained by donations and guided by Christian compassion, humility, and service to Westmoreland County, Indiana County, and the surrounding areas.

Angel Arms · Brighter Path

"At Angel Arms Brighter Path, our mission is to empower children who face trauma and special challenges — including those affected by Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (NAS) and other drug exposure — by providing a supportive and nurturing environment."

We utilize the ITCM model to implement a holistic approach that addresses each child's educational, nutritional, social-emotional, counseling, and developmental needs — fostering resilience, building self-esteem, and creating brighter futures for every child in our care.

Called to Care

"Called to Care — National and International, under Faith Forward to Serve Those in Need — exists to mobilize and equip teams to serve with integrity, humility, and cultural sensitivity."

We are committed to meeting urgent needs and fostering lasting impact — locally and globally — through collaborative partnerships, holistic care, and the demonstration of Christ's love in action.

Our approach

Holistic care for the whole person — and the whole family.

We treat each person as exactly that: a person. Not a case, not a statistic. Care that addresses the physical, psychological, and spiritual dimensions of healing.

01
